Resources for Reducing Your Ecological Footprint

Here are some tips and resources to help you reduce your environmental impact:

Diet

The food system is responsible for more than ⅓ of global greenhouse emissions, with a majority coming from animal-based foods. Industrial agriculture contributes to large land use and deforestation. Consider adopting a plant-based diet, which requires fewer natural resources and is better for individual health.

  • Learn more about the impact of food production and consumption
  • Food waste and its links to greenhouse gases
  • Research on food waste and environmental impacts

Clothing/Consumption

Fast fashion is a major contributor to waste. To reduce environmental impact, consider secondhand shopping or supporting sustainable fashion brands using eco-friendly, plant-based materials.

  • Learn more about sustainable fashion
  • The environmental impact of clothing production

Energy Usage

Approximately 40% of the world’s annual energy consumption comes from buildings. To reduce energy consumption, consider switching to renewable energy sources and improving the energy efficiency of your home.

  • Energy use and environmental impacts
  • Electricity use in homes

Transportation

The transportation industry is responsible for 25% of global energy-related CO2 emissions. Consider using alternative transportation methods like public transport, carpooling, or switching to an electric vehicle.

  • Learn about transportation emissions

Waste Production

Food waste in the U.S. alone produces 170 million metric tons of CO2 emissions. Consider reducing food waste by purchasing only what you need and composting organic waste. Landfills are a significant source of methane emissions, and reducing waste helps decrease this impact.

Environmental Facts and Tips

Here are some important facts and statistics that shed light on environmental impact:

Food System

The food system is responsible for more than ⅓ of global greenhouse emissions, with a majority coming from animal-based foods. Industrial agriculture contributes to large land use and deforestation. Consider adopting a plant-based diet, which requires fewer natural resources and is better for individual health.

Waste Production

Total generation of municipal solid waste was 292.4 million tons, or 4.9 pounds per person per day. Reducing, reusing, and recycling can make a significant impact on reducing waste.

Clothing Consumption

The average US consumer throws away 81.5 lbs of clothes every year. Consider secondhand shopping or supporting sustainable fashion brands to help reduce waste.

Energy Usage

The average American household uses about 10,500 kilowatt hours of electricity a year. Switching to energy-efficient appliances and renewable energy sources can significantly reduce your carbon footprint.

Transportation

A typical passenger vehicle emits about 4.6 metric tons of CO2 per year. Using public transport, carpooling, or switching to electric vehicles can help reduce this impact.
